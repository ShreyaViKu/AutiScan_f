Resources & Support for Parents

Helping you navigate therapies, care, and support with confidence.

1. Diagnostic Tools

🩺 ADOS (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule)

A widely used standardized assessment conducted by professionals to evaluate social interaction, communication, and behavior patterns.

🔹Assesses social interaction, communication, and repetitive behaviours

🔹Helps identify strengths and developmental challenges

🔹Conducted in specialized clinics or child development centers

2. Therapies & Interventions

🧠 Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

🔹Helps children manage emotions and reduce anxiety

🔹Can be adapted using visual aids and structured sessions

👐 Occupational Therapy (OT)

🔹Focuses on daily life skills like dressing and handwriting

🔹Improves sensory processing and independence

🏃 Physiotherapy

🔹Supports balance, coordination, and movement

🔹Helpful for posture or motor challenges

3. Child Development Centres & Special Schools

🏫 Comprehensive support in one place

🔹Evaluate, diagnose, and provide multiple therapies under one roof.

🔹Offer specialized programs for social, academic, and motor skill development.

🔹Parents can choose integrated therapy programs for coordinated support.

4. Additional Support for Parents & Children

👪 Parent Training & Workshops

🔹Learn strategies to reinforce learning and behavior at home.

🎨 Interest-Based Activities

🔹Encourage hobbies like art, music, coding, or storytelling to enhance learning.

🤝 Peer Support Groups

🔹Connect with other parents and children on the spectrum for advice, activities, and emotional support.

5. At-Home Activities (Parent-Supervised)

🃏 Flashcards, puzzles, and matching games

🔹Can help children practice letters, numbers, shapes, colors, or social cues.

🔹Best done under parental supervision, ensuring the child understands the activity and stays engaged.

✂️ Craft, coloring, and storytelling activities

🔹Promote creativity, fine motor skills, and imagination.

🎵 Music and movement exercises

🔹Encourage rhythm, coordination, and sensory integration.

Tip: Always adapt activities to your child’s interests and comfort level. Even small daily sessions of 10–15 minutes can make a difference.
