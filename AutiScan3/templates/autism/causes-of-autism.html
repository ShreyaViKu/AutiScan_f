UNDERSTANDING ASD

What Causes Autism?

Current research points to a combination of genetic and environmental factors — not a single cause.

A Complex, Multi-Factor Origin

Researchers do not believe autism has a single cause. Instead, current evidence suggests autism results from a combination of genetic predisposition and certain environmental influences during early development.

Autism is not caused by parenting style, vaccines, or anything a parent did during pregnancy. These myths have been thoroughly debunked by large-scale scientific research.

Genetic Factors

Genetics play a significant role in autism. Studies show that autism runs in families, and identical twins are much more likely to both have autism than fraternal twins.

Researchers have identified hundreds of genes associated with autism risk. Some rare gene mutations strongly influence development, while many common genetic variants may combine to create susceptibility.

Higher risk if a sibling has autism
Identical twin concordance ~70–90%
Hundreds of associated gene variants
New (de novo) mutations also identified

Environmental Influences

While genetics play a major role, certain environmental factors during pregnancy and early development may increase risk in genetically susceptible individuals. These include:

Advanced parental age at time of conception
Prenatal exposure to certain medications
Complications during birth (oxygen deprivation)
Premature birth or low birth weight

These are risk factors — not causes. Most children with these factors do not develop autism.

What Does NOT Cause Autism

Scientific consensus is clear that the following do not cause autism:

❌ Childhood vaccines (MMR or others)
❌ Bad parenting or lack of emotional warmth
❌ Screen time or diet in early childhood
❌ Trauma or emotional stress alone
