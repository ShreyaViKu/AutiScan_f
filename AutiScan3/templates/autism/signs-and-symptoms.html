Signs & Symptoms of Autism

Understanding early signs helps families seek timely support and intervention.

When Do Signs Appear?

Signs of autism can often be detected before age 2–3. Some parents notice differences in their child's development as early as 12–18 months. In other cases, signs become more apparent once a child enters social settings like school.

Early recognition is important — but signs vary widely depending on the child's age, ability level, and individual profile.

Social Communication Signs

Limited or inconsistent eye contact
Doesn't respond to their name by 12 months
Difficulty sharing interests or enjoyment
Limited use of gestures (waving, pointing)
Prefers solitary play over group play
Difficulty understanding social rules

Communication & Language Signs

Delayed speech or language development
Repeating words or phrases (echolalia)
Difficulty starting or sustaining conversations
Unusual tone, rhythm, or pitch of speech
Difficulty understanding figurative language
May have extensive vocabulary but struggle socially

Behavior & Sensory Signs

Repetitive movements (hand flapping, rocking, spinning)
Strong attachment to routines — upset by changes
Intense, focused interest in specific topics
Over or under-sensitivity to sensory input
Unusual reactions to sounds, textures, or lights
Difficulty with transitions between activities

Important Reminder

Having one or even several of these signs does not mean a child has autism. Many traits appear in typical development. A formal diagnosis requires comprehensive evaluation by qualified professionals.

If you are concerned about your child's development, the best first step is to speak with your pediatrician or use a screening tool like AutiScan to help structure your observations.
